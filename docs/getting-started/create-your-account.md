# Create Your ShopNest Account

A ShopNest account links your cart, saves shipping addresses, records your order history, and unlocks the [loyalty program](/features/loyalty) and [wishlist](/features/wishlist). You can browse and fill a cart without one, but you sign in to check out.

## Ways to sign in

ShopNest offers three sign-in methods, all backed by Firebase Authentication:

- **Google** — one-tap sign-in with your Google account. On the web this uses a secure popup; on mobile it uses the native Google sign-in flow.
- **Apple** — sign in with your Apple ID, available on supported platforms.
- **Email and password** — create a classic account with your email address.

Whichever method you choose, you land as the same user, so you can return through any of them.

## Set up your profile

After your first sign-in, open your **dashboard** to complete your profile:

1. **Profile** — your name and contact details, used on orders and invoices.
2. **Addresses** — save one or more shipping addresses to speed up checkout.
3. **Security** — manage your sign-in and, where available, enable biometric re-login.

## Biometric re-login

On devices that support it, ShopNest can re-authenticate you with a fingerprint or face scan instead of re-entering credentials. This is optional, stored securely on the device, and never replaces your underlying account — it's a convenience layer over it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need an account to browse?

No. You can browse the catalog, search, compare products, and add items to your cart without signing in. An account is required only at checkout and to track orders.

### Can I switch sign-in methods later?

You sign in with the method tied to your account. If you created an account with email, continue using email; Google and Apple accounts likewise return through their providers.

### Where is my data stored?

Account and order data is stored in Firebase/Firestore. See the [privacy page](/privacy) for what's collected and how to [export or delete it](/guides/data-export-and-deletion).
